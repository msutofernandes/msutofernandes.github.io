Chapter 8 – Conducting the test session

The facilitator is responsible for guiding the participants, keep them moving, but not answering their questions, and encouraging them to think aloud or verbalize their thoughts. Here you have to remain neutral, don’t telling them what to do. And you have to hear their thoughts and understand what is confusing them.

After you set the room for the test, the first step is to ask the participant what they make of the home page, not their opinion. The next step is to begin with the specific tasks. If some participant is taking too long to finish a task or you realize you are not learning anything more, move to the next task. Always leave some time in the end to ask some questions to the participants, make them try to do a task again in another way, and follow up on any suggestions the participant made. Finish the test, thank and pay them.

When you face a participant that is not collaborating as expected, it’s okay to be persistent, after all, you’re paying for their time.
