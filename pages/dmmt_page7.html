Chapter 7 - Designing your Home page

The first few seconds a user spends on a new Web site is critical and the first page they usually see is the Home page. Your Home page needs to answer four questions:

  • What is this?
  • What can I do here?
  • What do they have here?
  • Why should I be here – and not somewhere else?

If any of those questions is not answered, most likely the user will get frustrated and leave your Web site.

There are three important places on the page where you can help the user understand what your site is about.

  • Tagline: Phrase next to the site ID (logo) as a short description of your Web site.
  • Welcome blurb: Terse description of your Web site, placed in a way that catches the user’s eye first.
  • Learn more: A more detailed explanation of your product or business, it can be a text or video.
